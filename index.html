Welcome to Lesbos. Read this carefully.

It is October 2015. You are a registration officer with the European Humanitarian Response Agency, stationed at a field office on the island of Lesbos, Greece.

Over 500,000 people have crossed the Aegean from Turkey to Greece this year. On some days more than 10,000 arrive on this island alone. Your office is part of an emergency response to a collapsed asylum system.

Your job is to process asylum claims. Each person who sits across this desk is asking for protection. Your decisions — who gets registered, deferred, rejected — have real consequences for real lives.

You will process 10 cases in a single shift. You have 10 minutes total. Some cases are straightforward. Many are not.

The 1951 Refugee Convention

A refugee is a person with a well-founded fear of persecution for one of five grounds, unable or unwilling to return to their country.

The five grounds
Race  ·  Religion  ·  Nationality  ·  Political opinion  ·  Membership of a particular social group

Armed conflict is accepted as grounds when it produces well-founded fear on one of those bases. Syrians, Afghans, and Eritreans frequently qualify.

⚠ Critical rule
Absence of documents does not disqualify a claim. UNHCR Handbook §195: you may not reject solely because an applicant lacks identity documents. Many people fleeing persecution cannot bring papers.

Some nationalities are designated safe countries of origin — presumption against refugee status, but not automatic rejection.

2015 EU Temporary Protection & Your Protocols

On 14 September 2015, the EU Council activated a Temporary Protection Directive for Syrian nationals. Fast-track registration applies — presumption of eligibility.

Your four decision options
Register — criteria met, case proceeds.
Defer — needs verification. Costs 30s of shift time.
Reject — clearly does not qualify. Must be legally justified.
Escalate — beyond your authority: trafficking, security, complex legal edge. Costs 45s.
⚠ Critical rule
Welfare flags are mandatory and separate from your decision. Flag unaccompanied minors, unwell children, trafficking indicators, family separation, and age disputes regardless of whether you register or reject. Missing a required flag is recorded in your debrief and costs welfare score.

Your shift begins in ten minutes.

10 cases. 10 minutes. No feedback until the end.

Each case: a testimony · documents · the legal framework · your decision + welfare flags

At the end of your shift you will see a full debrief: your decisions vs. the legally defensible outcomes, missed welfare flags, and what happened to three of the people you processed today.

⏰ About the timer
If time runs out before you finish all 10 cases, the shift ends and you go straight to the debrief. Defer costs 30s. Escalate costs 45s. You have a 2-second window to undo any accidental decision.

The people waiting outside this tent are real. The framework in front of you is real. The decisions are yours.
